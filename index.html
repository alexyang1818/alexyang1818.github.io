Hybrid Modeling Using RNN

Mechanistic models derived from laboratory experiments partly lose their accuracy when applied to manufacturing reactors. This is because of imperfect mixing and mass transfer limits at a larger scale. Manufacturing reactors are often operated under different conditions than laboratory experiments, which also potentially reduces the accuracy of mechanistic models. In this project, I developed a hybrid model that uses a many-to-one RNN model to accurately predict the final concentration of a batch reaction. The RNN model is added with a masking layer, which makes the RNN model a dynamic RNN model, so that it is able to evaluate batch reactions with varying batch time. Additionally, to properly scale data of chemical concentrations, which can change across many orders of magnitude, I added a scaler for log transformation.
